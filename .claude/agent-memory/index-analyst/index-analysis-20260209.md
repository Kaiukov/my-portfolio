## Market Sentiment Assessment

### Risk Regime: RISK-OFF with Selective Risk-On Pockets

The market exhibits **classic risk-off characteristics** despite some positive closes:

- **Global Capital Flight Pattern:** US tech sector suffering while defensive/value sectors rally
- **Safe-Haven Geography:** Europe (DAX, FTSE) outperforming US = capital flow toward real economy
- **Asia Recovery Narrative:** Hong Kong breaking higher on tech/bank support, despite global software fears

### Risk-Off Indicators (DOMINANT)

1. **Software Sector Apocalypse** (~$1 trillion wiped from sector)
   - iShares Expanded Tech-Software ETF down 28% from recent highs, 17% YTD
   - Specific victims: Intuit -11%, Salesforce -7%, ServiceNow -7%
   - Narrative driver: AI automation tools (including Claude) disrupting traditional SaaS models

2. **Tech-Heavy Nasdaq Weakness** (-0.46%)
   - Below S&P 500 performance = concentration risk materializing
   - Indicates growth/momentum rotation away from expensive tech names

3. **Labor Market Deterioration**
   - January 2026: 108,435 layoffs (highest since 2008 financial crisis)
   - Suggests profit cycle pressure from AI efficiency demands
   - Risk sentiment response: reduce leverage, rotate to stability

4. **Volatility Regime Shift**
   - VIX at 18.64 (February average) - elevated from pre-crisis 16-17 baseline
   - Seasonal February volatility spike +5.5% typical, and we're seeing it manifest
   - Spikes likely as macro data + earnings miss targets

### Selective Risk-On Counters (MINOR)

1. **Real Economy Resilience**
   - Dow Jones at new ATH (50,219) = industrial/financials benefiting from US strength
   - Small caps (Russell 2000 +0.89%) = domestic economy signal

2. **Value Rotation Signal**
   - Dow outperformance vs NASDAQ suggests shift from growth → value
   - Infrastructure/materials/energy hitting all-time highs
   - Metals recovering (Palantir AI easing fears per market commentary)

3. **Asia Tech Recovery**
   - Hong Kong +1.73% on tech/bank strength

   - Suggests correction may be consolidation, not collapse
