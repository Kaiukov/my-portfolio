This is a classic 'over the cliff' scenario where long-term investors must evaluate management's execution in rebuilding the portfolio, making it a genuine long-term signal requiring continued monitoring.

    Stripping out certain one-time items, adjusted per-share earnings were $2.04, ahead of the $2.01 anticipated by analysts, according to FactSet.

   Revenue rose 5% to $16.4 billion. Analysts surveyed by FactSet forecast revenue of $16.20 billion.

          Merck said sales were driven by growth in its oncology business, as well as cardiometabolic and respiratory, despite a decline in vaccines.

   Revenue from Keytruda rose 7% to $8.37 billion in the quarter, driven by an increased uptake in earlier-stage indications of the cancer immunotherapy. Sales of the HPV vaccine Gardasil meanwhile fell 34% to $1.03 billion due to lower demand in China and lower sales in Japan.

   In 2026, Merck expects revenue to be $65.5 billion to $67 billion. Adjusted earnings per share are guided to be $5 to $5.15.

   Analysts were projecting $67.44 billion in annual sales and $5.27 in adjusted earnings per share.

   Merck said its adjusted earnings forecast includes a one-time charge of about $9 billion related to the acquisition of the antiviral maker Cidara. That translates to about $3.65 a share, plus 30 cents a share from financing and operational costs.

          Merck announced several merger deals last year as it braces for Keytruda to lose the protection of its main U.S. patent. The patent expires in 2028, opening the door for lower-cost versions to compete with one of Merck’s biggest revenue drivers.

   Along with announcing its Cidara deal last year, Merck agreed to pay about $10 billion for Verona Pharma and its lung drug.

   Merck also said Tuesday it is set to get some relief from tariffs. It came to an agreement with the U.S. government that is intended to lower medicine costs for Americans in exchange for a pause on higher import duties.

   Under the agreement, Merck will provide products through a direct-to-patient program at affordable prices. In return, the U.S. government will delay Section 232 tariffs for three years.

    Merck said it has committed more than $70 billion in capital and R&amp;D spending toward strengthening U.S. production and development.
