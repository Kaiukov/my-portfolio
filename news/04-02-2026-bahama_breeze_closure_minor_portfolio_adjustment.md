**Scale of Impact**: Bahama Breeze represents only ~1.3% of Darden's total restaurant count (28 of ~2,159 locations) and approximately 2% of revenue ($247M of $12.58B total sales). Company explicitly stated: "does not expect these actions to have a material impact on financial results."

**Economic Moat Filter**: No impact. Darden's competitive advantage rests on core brands (Olive Garden, LongHorn Steakhouse, Cheddar's, Ruth's Chris, Yard House, etc.). Removing an underperforming minor brand actually strengthens focus on higher-margin concepts.

**Macro/Structural Filter**: No structural impact. This is company-specific operational optimization, not a signal about casual dining sector or broader economy. Other Darden brands continue performing well.

**Value vs. Price Filter**: Value intact. Bahama Breeze sales declined 7.7% YoY; 15 locations already closed in May 2025. This is disciplined capital allocation, not systemic decline. Converting prime locations to stronger brands may improve margins.

**Strategic Context**: Darden completed a formal strategic review (announced June 2025) and chose conversion over sale. This reflects proactive portfolio management following their $649M Chuy's acquisition.

**Investor Action**: Long-term investors should treat this as routine portfolio optimization. The stock has gained 13% in 2026 (fourth straight yearly gain), demonstrating market confidence in core operations. Monitor Q2 earnings for conversion costs, but this doesn't alter the investment thesis.

[*  ](https://www.globaldata.com/)  # Darden Restaurants to shut half of Bahama Breeze sites in US
             Umesh Ellichipuram   Wed, February 4, 2026 at 1:19 PM GMT+2 1 min read                           Darden Restaurants has announced plans to close 14 of Bahama Breeze’s 28 US locations following the completion of a strategic review of the business.

   The company plans to convert the remaining 14 sites to other brands within the Darden portfolio.

   Darden had previously stated that Bahama Breeze sites were “no longer a strategic priority” and that it would consider “strategic alternatives”, including a potential sale of the brand.

          Locations scheduled to close include restaurants in several US states, including Delaware, Georgia, Michigan, New Jersey, North Carolina, and Virginia.

   The closure list also includes restaurants in Florida, along with sites in King of Prussia and Pittsburgh in Pennsylvania.

   These restaurants marked for permanent closure are expected to continue operating until 5 April 2026.

   Darden plans to convert the remaining 14 Bahama Breeze locations over the next 12 to 18 months. These locations are expected to remain open until temporary closures are needed to complete the conversion work.

   The company has not yet disclosed which of its brands will take over those sites.

   However, it believes “the conversion locations are great sites that will benefit several of the brands in its portfolio”.

          Darden does not expect the closures and conversions to have a material impact on its financial results.

   It added that its priority “will continue to be on supporting team members, including placing as many as possible in roles within the Darden portfolio.”
