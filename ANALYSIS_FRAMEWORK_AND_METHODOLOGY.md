## Complete Confidence Scoring System

### Weighted Formula
```
CONFIDENCE SCORE = 
  (Source Reliability × 0.30) +
  (Clarity & Specificity × 0.25) +
  (Directness of Impact × 0.25) +
  (Consensus & Conflict Factors × 0.20)
```

## Tier-Based Source Reliability Scoring

### Tier 1: Official Sources (90-100%)
**Primary Sources**: Bloomberg, Reuters, UN agencies (OCHA, UNICEF, WHO), EU Council, Ukrainian Government, IEA

**Characteristics**:
- Multiple independent verification points
- Institutional backing
- Transparent methodology
- Peer review/fact-checking
- Long track record

**Score**: 95%

### Tier 2: Established Media & Professional Analysis (70-89%)
**Primary Sources**: Kyiv Independent, Euronews, Deloitte, Euractiv, Financial Times

**Characteristics**:
- Reputable news organization
- Editorial review process
- Industry expertise
- Some verification requirements
- Occasional bias risk

**Score**: 85%

### Tier 3: Secondary Sources & Analysts (50-69%)
**Primary Sources**: Industry analysts, specialized reports, think tanks

**Characteristics**:
- Interpretive layer added
- Variable verification standards
- Expertise present but concentrated
- Potential conflicts of interest
- Narrower editorial oversight

**Score**: 60%

### Tier 4: Unverified/Opinion Sources (Below 50%)
**Avoid**: Russian sources, unverified social media, speculation

## News Clarity & Specificity Scoring

### High (85-100%)
- Specific numbers included (e.g., "$67B energy rebuild", "60% capacity")
- Clear causality stated
- Quantifiable impact measurable
- Multiple data points supporting claim
- Recent date stamp

**Example**: "Ukraine's energy capacity at 60% of demand; $67B reconstruction package confirmed by EU Council"
**Score**: 95%

### Medium (60-84%)
- General statements with some detail
- Moderate specificity levels
- Causal chain implied but not explicit
- Partially quantifiable
- Slightly dated information acceptable

**Example**: "International reconstruction funding proceeding; energy sector urgently needs support"
**Score**: 70%

### Low (30-59%)
- Vague claims without numbers
- Interpretive language
- Limited factual basis
- Causality unclear
- Older information applied to current situation

**Example**: "Ukraine will need significant reconstruction"
**Score**: 40%

### Very Low (0-29%)
- Speculative language ("may", "could", "might")
- Multiple interpretations possible
- Anecdotal evidence
- No concrete data
- Highly dependent on assumptions

**Example**: "Some experts think peace talks might lead to reconstruction"
**Score**: 15%

## Directness of Impact Scoring

### Direct Impact (90-100%)
**Definition**: Immediate, measurable effect on revenue, earnings, margins, or operations

**Example**: "60% energy capacity forces immediate equipment procurement"
- Direct cause: Energy crisis
- Direct effect: Equipment orders
- Timing: Q1 2026 (immediate)
- Magnitude: $67B+ market

**Score**: 95%

### Moderately Direct (70-89%)
**Definition**: Clear connection but requires time to materialize or one intermediate step

**Example**: "EU reconstruction funding enables infrastructure projects"
- Direct cause: Funding commitment
- Intermediate step: Regulatory framework creation
- Effect: Infrastructure projects begin
- Timing: 6-12 months (medium-term)

**Score**: 80%

### Indirect (50-69%)
**Definition**: Secondary effects; requires chain of assumptions

**Example**: "Peace talks might improve investor sentiment toward Ukraine"
- Direct cause: Peace talks
- Assumption 1: Positive outcome
- Assumption 2: Market interprets positively
- Assumption 3: Capital deployment follows
- Effect: Investment increases
- Timing: 12+ months

**Score**: 55%

### Negligible (0-49%)
**Definition**: Tangential or theoretical impact

**Example**: "Climate change may affect long-term agricultural productivity"
- Cause: Climate change
- Multiple intervening variables
- Impact: Theoretical and distant
- Timing: 10+ years

**Score**: 25%

## Consensus & Conflict Factor Scoring

### Consensus (+10 points bonus)
**Definition**: Multiple reputable sources align on same interpretation

**Example**:
- IEA confirms 60% energy capacity
- UN OCHA confirms 10.8M humanitarian need
- EU Council confirms EUR 682B package
- Ukrainian Government confirms targets

**Application**: All sources align on energy crisis reality
**Score Adjustment**: +0% to +10% (adds to base consensus confidence)

### Contrarian (0 points)
**Definition**: Single source or minority view among experts

**Example**:
- One analyst predicts 50% probability ceasefire within 6 months
- Other sources give 55-60% probability

**Application**: Minority view gets baseline score but no consensus bonus
**Score Adjustment**: 0% (no bonus)

### Active Debate (-10 points penalty)
**Definition**: Significant disagreement among credible experts

**Example**:
- Some sources: Ceasefire probability 40%
- Other sources: Ceasefire probability 70%
- Fundamental disagreement on causality

**Application**: Reduces confidence score due to uncertainty
**Score Adjustment**: -0% to -10% (reduces from base score)

## The Four-Filter Investment Framework

### Filter 1: Economic Moat Analysis
**Question**: Does this news fundamentally change market structure, competitive positioning, or profitability?

**What We Evaluate**:
- Market share dynamics
- Barriers to entry/exit
- Competitive advantages
- Pricing power
- Margin sustainability

**Example - Energy Sector**:
- Current state: 60% capacity = insufficient supply
- Moat creation: Equipment vendors gain pricing power (scarcity)
- Duration: Until capacity returns to 100% (6-24 months)
- Verdict: POSITIVE - Temporary but real margin expansion

**Score Impact**: High-conviction economic moat = higher confidence

### Filter 2: Macro/Structural Impact Analysis
**Question**: Does this change the broader economic framework in which markets operate?

**What We Evaluate**:
- Regulatory environment changes
- Industry supply chain restructuring
- Economic policy shifts
- Interest rate/capital availability changes
- Long-term structural shifts

**Example - Reconstruction Funding**:
- Current state: EUR 682B committed by EU/US/multilateral institutions
- Structural impact: 10-year demand certainty regardless of political outcome
- Framework: Survives frozen conflict (doesn't require perfect peace)
- Verdict: POSITIVE - Creates structural long-term opportunity

**Score Impact**: Structural shift = decade-long thesis validity

### Filter 3: Value vs. Price Analysis
**Question**: Is the market reacting emotionally to missed expectations, or does the price movement reflect fundamental value changes?

**What We Evaluate**:
- Whether news is already priced in
- Market's historical reaction patterns
- Analyst consensus expectations
- Valuation metric changes needed
- Margin of safety for investors

**Example - Reconstruction Package**:
- News: EUR 682B confirmed (expected)
- Market reaction: Already priced; infrastructure stocks flat
- Verdict: NEWS CORRECTLY PRICED - Validates thesis but doesn't reprice market
- Implication: Long-term opportunity clear, not short-term catalyst

**Score Impact**: Correctly priced news = long-term signal, not short-term trade

### Filter 4: War Impact Analysis (Ukraine-Specific)
**Question**: How does this news change war timeline, ceasefire probability, or reconstruction scope?

**What We Evaluate**:
- War damage vs. reconstruction rate
- Ceasefire economic incentives
- Humanitarian leverage for peace
- Defense implications
- Reconstruction demand certainty

**Example - Largest Aerial Assault (450 drones, 71 missiles)**:
- War damage: Destroying $5B+ infrastructure per major assault
- Reconstruction demand: $67B+ energy rebuild guaranteed
- Ceasefire incentive: Russia destroying faster than Ukraine rebuilds; both sides economically motivated toward resolution
- Verdict: POSITIVE FOR RECONSTRUCTION THESIS - War damage validates $67B+ need

**Score Impact**: War damage confirmation = validates reconstruction thesis (not negative)

## Confidence Threshold: 88% Minimum Rule

**Rationale**:
- Below 88%: High noise-to-signal ratio
- At 88%+: Actionable signals with sufficient clarity
- 92%+: High-conviction signals worthy of portfolio allocation
- 95%+: Exceptional signals with multiple reinforcing evidence

**Application**:
- Score <88%: IGNORE (noise filter)
- Score 88-91%: MONITOR (developing signals)
- Score 92-94%: ACT (high-conviction signals)
- Score 95%+: PRIORITIZE (exceptional signals)

**Rationale for 88% threshold**: 
Balances false positive risk (accepting noise) against false negative risk (rejecting valid signals). Lower threshold risks allocation to noise; higher threshold risks missing valid opportunities.

## Noise vs. Signal Distinction Framework

### NOISE (Ignore)
1. **Daily peace talk updates** - Diplomatic theater without outcomes
2. **Individual casualty reports** - Humanitarian context, not economic signal
3. **Spot attacks/outages** - Part of systematic pattern already analyzed
4. **Political statements** - Ignore unless backed by capital deployment
5. **Currency fluctuations** - Short-term noise, macro forces
6. **Sentiment-driven moves** - Emotional reaction without fundamental change
7. **Analyst conference calls** - Unless backed by order data

### SIGNAL (Monitor)
1. **Specific capacity metrics** - 60% capacity = actionable data
2. **Procurement orders** - Real capital deployment
3. **Funding commitments** - Binding institutional capital
4. **Destruction metrics** - War damage quantification
5. **Humanitarian scale** - Ceasefire probability proxy
6. **Project announcements** - Execution indicators
7. **Institutional involvement** - Capital deployment signals
