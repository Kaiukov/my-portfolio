UBS Q4 2025 earnings confirm durable structural improvements from the Credit Suisse integration. Underlying pre-tax profit rose 62% YoY to CHF 2.9B, revenues grew 10%, and the firm increased its gross cost-savings ambition from roughly CHF 13B to CHF 13.5B, with cumulative run-rate savings of CHF 10.7B already delivered. About 85% of Swiss client migrations were completed by year-end, de-risking the integration and positioning further savings in 2026. Capital is strong (CET1 14.4%), and the bank raised the dividend 22% to .10 while reiterating a B 2026 buyback, with additional returns contingent on maintaining ~14% CET1 and clarity on Swiss regulatory reforms. Wealth Management and Investment Bank showed solid momentum: GWM pre-tax profit increased to CHF 1.6B with CHF 101B net new assets in 2025, while IB delivered record annual revenue of CHF 11.8B with no incremental RWA. Although the quarter included one-time items (PPA adjustments, litigation accruals) and some analyst chatter around an EPS miss, the core results demonstrate sustainable post-merger scale and efficiency gains. For long-term investors, the key takeaway is that UBS is successfully executing a complex integration, expanding structural cost saves, and delivering broad-based revenue and capital strength—materially improving its competitive position and returns profile. The primary external dependency remains the timing and details of Swiss regulatory reforms, but even there UBS has kept flexibility on dividends and buybacks. Overall, this is evidence of enduring business improvement rather than quarterly noise.
