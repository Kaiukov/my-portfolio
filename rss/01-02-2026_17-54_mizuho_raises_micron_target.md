This analyst revision reflects a fundamental multi-year transformation in the memory industry driven by structural supply-demand imbalances. Key long-term indicators include: (1) Projections extending through 2027 with sequential price increases (330% in 2026, 50% in 2027), (2) Supply constraints expected to persist with no new NAND wafer capacity in 2026-2027, (3) CEO statement that memory markets will remain 'tight past 2026,' (4) AI-driven demand creating sustained market pressure rather than temporary fluctuation, (5) EPS expansion of 275% expected over multiple years. These factors point to a structural 'supercycle' in memory, not a short-term price adjustment. The combination of constrained supply, accelerating AI demand, and multi-year pricing power indicates lasting market repositioning for Micron rather than ephemeral market conditions.
