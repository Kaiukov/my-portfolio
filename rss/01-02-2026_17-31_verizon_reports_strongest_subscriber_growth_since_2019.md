Verizons results indicate a fundamental turnaround rather than temporary gains. The Frontier acquisition expanded fiber footprint to 30 million homes, creating lasting infrastructure advantage. Strategic restructuring under new CEO including 13,000 job cuts, operational streamlining, and shift to volume-based growth model represent fundamental business changes. The company reached a critical inflection point with broadband base now at 16.3 million and fixed wireless access at 5.7 million subscribers, establishing long-term competitive positioning. While near-term profitability declined with margins contracting and net income halving, the 2026 guidance forecasting 7% free cash flow growth to highest levels since 2020 indicates management expects these gains to be sustainable. The aggressive promotions may initially drive customer acquisition, but the expanded fiber and wireless infrastructure combined with leaner operations create enduring competitive advantages that should outlast short-term promotional effects.
