The article argues AI will have profound and lasting economic impact, drawing explicit parallels to historic technological revolutions like electrification in the 1920s and the internet boom of the 1990s. The text emphasizes that while current AI-exposed stocks may experience a bubble correction (short-term volatility), the technology itself will create transformative long-term changes across industries. Key evidence includes: 1) Historical precedent where electricity and internet took years to realize productivity booms but ultimately reshaped entire economies over decades; 2) Small-cap and old-economy companies expected to gain permanent earnings power from efficiency improvements; 3) The J-curve productivity pattern requiring upfront investment before transformative benefits emerge; 4) Economic value flowing downstream to companies employing AI rather than just building it, similar to how electrified manufacturers outperformed during the Great Depression and internet-leveraging businesses thrived after the dotcom crash. The article's core thesis is that bubbles are a natural part of epochal technological shifts, and the fundamental changes AI brings will be measured in decades, not months.
