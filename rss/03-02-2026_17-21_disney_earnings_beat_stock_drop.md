Disney reported solid Q1 2026 earnings, beating analyst expectations with EPS of .63 (+bash.05 above estimates) and revenue of 6B (+5.3% YoY, +00M beat). Despite these positive results, the stock dropped 7% due to: 1) CEO succession uncertainty (Bob Iger's expected departure before end of 2026), 2) concerns about international theme park visitors, and 3) weak forward guidance. 

Applying the three filters:

**Economic Moat Filter**: No fundamental change to Disney's competitive position. The company's core moats remain intact: world-class IP portfolio (Disney, Pixar, Marvel, Star Wars), global theme parks infrastructure, streaming platforms, and unparalleled brand equity. The earnings beat demonstrates operational strength.

**Macro/Structural Filter**: This is purely company-specific news about governance transition. No broader economic or regulatory impact. The succession process is orderly with an internal successor (Josh D'Amaro, head of Disney's most profitable division).

**Value vs. Price Filter**: Clear case of emotional market reaction. The company exceeded financial expectations yet was punished 7% for transitional concerns (CEO succession) and temporary issues (quarterly guidance, park visitor fluctuations). The 10M YouTube TV carriage dispute is a one-time event, not systemic margin decline.

The market is pricing in transition uncertainty rather than reflecting fundamental business deterioration. Long-term investors should view this as an opportunity rather than a threat.
