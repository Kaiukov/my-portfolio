This proposed VAT registration threshold change (effective January 2027) represents a fundamental structural tax reform with profound long-term consequences: (1) Permanently alters tax obligations for 660,000 small businesses representing 40% of Ukraine's entrepreneurs; (2) Creates enduring administrative cost burdens estimated at 150-180 billion hryvnias annually, far exceeding the 40 billion hryvnias in projected revenue; (3) May cause permanent business restructuring, mass closures, or forced fragmentation of small enterprises (120,000 FOPs closed in Q1 2025 alone); (4) Structural shift in competitiveness from small to large businesses with lasting economic distribution changes; (5) Ongoing IMF conditionality affecting Ukraine's long-term fiscal framework; (6) Permanent consumer price increases as VAT and administrative costs are embedded in pricing; (7) Unlike temporary measures, this legislation creates a new baseline for small business taxation with no sunset provision, fundamentally reshaping Ukraine's small business ecosystem for the foreseeable future.
