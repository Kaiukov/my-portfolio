The article argues TSMC represents a long-term infrastructure play that will endure regardless of AI market cycles. TSMC holds a virtual monopoly on advanced chip manufacturing, serving multiple durable sectors including smartphones (Apple), GPUs (Nvidia), automotive (Tesla), and networking (Broadcom). The high barriers to entry in semiconductor manufacturing create a defensible moat. Even if the AI boom proves overheated, TSMC's diversified customer base across foundational technology sectors ensures sustained relevance and revenue growth, making it a permanent infrastructure layer rather than a temporary trend play.
