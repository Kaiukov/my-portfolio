This article describes a structural feature of the Vanguard Dividend Appreciation ETF (VIG) - its focus on dividend growth rather than high yield, which allows it to hold technology companies like Broadcom, Microsoft, Apple, and Mastercard that typically have low dividend yields but strong dividend growth records. This represents an enduring investment strategy that makes dividend ETFs accessible to growth-oriented and AI-focused investors, potentially changing how younger investors approach dividend investing. The 13% average earnings growth rate of holdings and the ETF's permanent allocation to tech sector giants make this a long-term portfolio construction consideration, not a temporary market event.
