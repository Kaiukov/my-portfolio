This article discusses Microsoft's fundamental long-term positioning in AI infrastructure. The 25 billion residual performance obligations (RPO) represents contractual future revenue that provides strong visibility for sustained growth. The current Azure growth deceleration is due to GPU supply constraints and strategic resource allocation to Copilot products, not lack of demand. Microsoft is prioritizing long-term AI distribution (Microsoft 365 Copilot, GitHub Copilot, Security Copilot) over short-term Azure metrics, which Morgan Stanley characterizes as 'strategic discipline.' With 15 million paying Copilot seats, 10x year-over-year daily active user growth, and 80% of CIOs expecting Copilot adoption within 12 months, Microsoft is building enduring AI monetization capabilities. The 21x CY27 valuation is argued to underappreciate Microsoft's long-lasting growth potential, margin expansion opportunities, and revenue visibility from backlog.
